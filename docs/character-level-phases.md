# Character Level Phase Plan

Purpose: define canonically grounded story phases for Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 image generation. These phases are not UI labels; they are narrative anchors for prompts so each level has a distinct visual identity.

Accuracy rule: do not merge later-story traits into earlier phases. Example: Lục Vân Tiên is not blind during the Kiều Nguyệt Nga rescue; blindness belongs to a later ordeal phase.

## Phase Table

| Character | Work | Level 1 Phase | Level 2 Phase | Level 3 Phase | Canon / Prompt Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Chí Phèo | *Chí Phèo* - Nam Cao | **Before full dehumanization:** a poor, ordinary canh điền with buried capacity for lương thiện; visually modest, rural, vulnerable, not yet the village terror. | **Tha hóa after prison:** returns to Vũ Đại changed by prison and Bá Kiến's machinery; scarred, drunk, isolated, used as a tool of intimidation. | **Awakening and dead end:** after Thị Nở's care and the bowl of onion porridge, he wants to return to honest life, then confronts the impossibility with Bá Kiến. | Use the arc innocent poor laborer -> social dehumanization -> brief human awakening and tragic protest. Avoid making Level 3 merely “more violent”; its upgrade is moral consciousness and tragic intensity. |
| Mị | *Vợ chồng A Phủ* - Tô Hoài | **Young Mị before/at the start of oppression:** beautiful Hmong girl with youth, flute, love, and latent vitality, before the debt-wife life fully deadens her. | **Con dâu gạt nợ / spring-night awakening:** trapped in Pá Tra's house, outwardly silent, then stirred by spring, wine, flute, and the desire to go out before A Sử ties her. | **Cắt dây cứu A Phủ:** sees A Phủ's tear, recognizes her own suffering in him, cuts the rope, and flees toward life. | The level progression should be vitality -> buried vitality reawakening -> action for freedom. Avoid portraying Mị as already liberated before the A Phủ rescue phase. |
| Xuân Tóc Đỏ | *Số đỏ* - Vũ Trọng Phụng | **Street hustler origin:** lower-class, red-haired, opportunistic outsider; tennis-ball picker / streetwise nobody before entering the “Âu hóa” world. | **Social climber in the reformist bourgeois circle:** absorbed into Văn Minh's world, misread as modern, medical, sporting, civilized; benefits from absurd misunderstandings. | **Publicly inflated celebrity:** treated as a great figure by a corrupt, status-hungry society; satire peaks as fraud becomes honor. | His “upgrade” is satirical social inflation, not moral growth. Preserve comic ugliness and opportunism; do not turn him into a sincere hero. |
| Lục Vân Tiên | *Lục Vân Tiên* - Nguyễn Đình Chiểu | **Nghĩa hiệp rescue:** young scholar-hero on the road, not blind yet, fights Phong Lai's robbers and rescues Kiều Nguyệt Nga while keeping lễ and refusing reward. | **Hoạn nạn after blindness:** later suffers blindness and betrayal, but keeps đạo nghĩa and self-command through misfortune. | **Vindicated moral hero:** after trials, virtue is recognized; justice, loyalty, and righteous order are restored. | Critical correction: do not show blindness in Level 1 rescue imagery. Blindness can appear from Level 2 onward if we anchor that level in the later ordeal phase. |
| Thúy Kiều | *Truyện Kiều* - Nguyễn Du | **Tài sắc and first love:** gifted young woman before catastrophe, bound to family, talent, and love for Kim Trọng. | **Trao duyên / bán mình:** chooses chữ hiếu over chữ tình, gives her love to Thúy Vân, and enters the path of exile and sacrifice. | **Lầu Ngưng Bích and drifting fate:** isolated, remembering Kim Trọng and parents, sensing the storms of a long, uncertain fate. | Kiều’s level progression should move from luminous talent -> sacrificial rupture -> deep interior exile. Avoid making Level 3 a simple “royal upgrade”; the power is lyric sorrow and self-awareness. |
| Lão Hạc | *Lão Hạc* - Nam Cao | **Lonely father and Cậu Vàng:** poor old farmer preserving his son’s garden and living with Cậu Vàng as companion and memory of his son. | **After selling Cậu Vàng:** grief, guilt, and self-reproach after selling the dog; moral pain is stronger than material poverty. | **Final self-sacrifice:** entrusts money/garden to ông giáo and chooses death to preserve dignity and his son’s inheritance. | Progression is poverty with dignity -> moral wound -> tragic self-preservation of dignity. Do not depict him as greedy or comic; his choices come from love and self-respect. |
| Chị Dậu | *Tắt đèn* - Ngô Tất Tố | **Mùa sưu and desperate endurance:** poor mother/wife under tax pressure, nursing sick anh Dậu, trying to protect the family through pleading and sacrifice. | **Selling child/dog and rising pressure:** pushed to the edge by sưu thuế, hunger, and humiliation; visible exhaustion and desperation. | **Tức nước vỡ bờ:** when cai lệ threatens anh Dậu, she shifts from pleading to direct resistance and fights back. | Her “upgrade” is not status; it is pressure becoming resistance. Keep the family-protection motive central. |
| Ông Sáu | *Chiếc lược ngà* - Nguyễn Quang Sáng | **Return from war to meet Thu:** soldier-father comes home on leave with a scarred face, longing for recognition from his daughter. | **The late father-child recognition:** Thu finally calls him “ba” at the moment he must leave, turning joy into painful separation. | **Chiếc lược ngà at the base:** pours fatherly love into making the ivory comb and entrusts it before death. | Progression should be longing -> recognized love too late -> love preserved in the comb. Do not over-militarize; the emotional object is fatherhood. |
| Ông Hai | *Làng* - Kim Lân | **Proud villager in evacuation:** loves and boasts about làng Chợ Dầu, follows resistance news, carries village pride into tản cư life. | **Hearing the village followed the enemy:** shame, shock, silence, fear for family dignity; inner conflict between love of village and loyalty to resistance. | **News corrected / village vindicated:** rejoices even though his house was burned, because the village’s resistance honor is restored. | Progression is local pride -> painful ethical test -> patriotic joy and restored communal honor. Avoid portraying the false rumor as true. |
| Vũ Nương | *Chuyện người con gái Nam Xương* - Nguyễn Dữ | **Virtuous wife and mother:** gentle, faithful, manages household, cares for mother-in-law and child while Trương Sinh is away. | **The shadow misunderstanding:** the innocent “father shadow” for bé Đản becomes Trương Sinh's accusation; she is denied trust and a fair hearing. | **Hoàng Giang and vindication:** jumps into the river to preserve honor; later appears in the miraculous vindication but cannot return to earthly life. | Progression is virtue -> wrongful suspicion -> tragic vindication. Use the shadow, river, lamp/altar, and distance from the human world as phase anchors. |

## Image Generation Implications

- Level 1 should not always mean “weak”; it means the earliest or baseline canonical phase.
- Level 2 should show a real narrative transition, not just more decoration.
- Level 3 should show the strongest canonical/emotional phase, not arbitrary fantasy escalation.
- If a phase is tragic, the visual “upgrade” should come from composition, symbolism, lighting, and emotional stakes rather than making the character look richer or more powerful.
